We started PrivacySnapshot because tracking is out of control

Two years ago, while checking a news site, I opened my browser’s developer tools and saw more than 80 requests to ad servers, analytics platforms, and data brokers – all without my permission. That moment felt like a punch. I had no idea my “private” browsing was anything but private.

Most people don't have the time or technical knowledge to spot hidden trackers, let alone understand what fingerprinting means. That’s why I started building a small Chrome extension that could scan a page and show everything in plain English. No jargon. No fake privacy promises.

“I’m a solo developer who values honest tools. PrivacySnapshot is my attempt to make the web a little less creepy – one scan at a time.” – Sam, creator

Websites today use canvas fingerprinting, audioContext, WebGL renderer info, and hundreds of third‑party cookies to build shadow profiles. Our extension doesn't block anything by default – it simply educates you. Knowledge is the first step toward real privacy.

Our mission is to make privacy understandable for everyone, not just engineers. We don’t sell data, we don’t have investors breathing down our necks, and we never will. This is a small, sustainable project that puts people first.
